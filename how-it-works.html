How the workflow works

How RecordPathAI Works

RecordPathAI helps users move through the record clearing process step by step by collecting intake information, organizing case details, reviewing possible eligibility, and preparing a cleaner court packet workflow before filing.

From Intake to Filing Preparation

The platform is designed to take users from confusion and scattered information to a more organized, guided process that supports eligibility review and court packet preparation.

1

Start With Intake Information

The process begins with personal and case-related intake information. This gives RecordPathAI the foundation it needs to carry important details through the workflow and use them later in packet preparation.

Intake is meant to collect the core user information needed throughout the process, including the case state that controls which rules and court path apply.
This step reduces duplicate entry later by keeping the workflow organized from the beginning.
2

Enter and Organize Record Details

Next, users move into record details. This is where charges, case outcomes, dates, counts, and related filing information are organized in a more structured way so the platform can review the record more accurately.

RecordPathAI is being built for guided data entry with cleaner fields, organized case details, and a workflow that feels easier than starting with legal paperwork.
As the platform expands, this step supports better offense and disposition data for eligibility review, packet logic, and court-specific preparation.
3

Review Eligibility on the Packet Page

Eligibility review happens after the intake and record details are brought together on the packet page. That allows RecordPathAI to evaluate the record in a more complete context instead of making a decision too early.

This includes eligibility messaging, review flags, and timing-based guidance such as estimated eligibility dates where waiting periods apply.
Packet logic is designed to follow the case state so users are reviewed using the right rule set instead of the wrong jurisdiction.
4

Prepare the Filing Packet

Once the information is organized and reviewed, RecordPathAI helps prepare the filing packet workflow by bringing together the information needed for forms, summaries, and filing-related preparation.

The platform is being built to support document preparation, official-form workflows, and packet generation that is cleaner and faster than starting from scratch.
Where supported, this workflow can also include signature capture, packet review, and structured outputs that make the next step easier to understand.
5

Follow the Right Court Path

After the packet is prepared, RecordPathAI helps users understand what comes next. Depending on the court and state, that may include printing, signing, mailing, filing in person, or reviewing additional local requirements before submission.

The platform is being expanded to support court guidance, filing checklists, lookup helpers, and court-specific preparation so users have a clearer path instead of guessing.
Reminder features and checklist tools can also help users stay on track if they are not yet eligible or still need to complete additional steps before filing.
